## Decision Evidence

1. What is it? Forensic Evidence Intelligence for Family Law.
2. Who is it for? Family-law matters requiring attorney or qualified professional review.
3. What does it transform? Fragmented messages, documents, images, recordings, and case records.
4. Why trust it? Originals remain distinct; findings are bound to citations, exact source locators, and evidence identities.
5. What next? Request Controlled Pilot Access.

Result: Pass.

### Investor test

The hero and next product section show painful fragmented inputs, identified and traceable outputs, citation/source identity as the defensibility mechanism, family law as the initial market, a larger evidence-infrastructure architecture, and honest controlled-pilot status.

Result: Pass.

### Attorney test

The proof chain exposes the required neutral finding, event, citation, excerpt, original file locator, EvidenceObject, hash, preservation status, and human-review/system boundaries. It explicitly avoids intent, credibility, diagnosis, legal conclusions, custody recommendations, and admissibility guarantees.

Result: Pass pending human legal-safety review.

### Parent test

The page frames organization and review without accusation, escalation, promises to win, or encouragement to collect evidence obsessively. The next step is limited, controlled, and subject to privacy and fit review.

Result: Pass pending human trauma-informed/legal-safety review.

### TruthTrace distinctiveness

The candidate's core visual language is the evidence relationship itself: identifiers, hashes, event links, exact source locators, preservation state, inspectable stages, and a claim export gate. This creates product-specific memory without relying on decorative spectacle.

Result: Pass.
